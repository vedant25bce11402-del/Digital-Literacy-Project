# Case Study: Phishing Attack

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted entity.
This crime usually occurs through emails, messages, or fake websites.
A typical phishing attack happens step-by-step as follows: First, the attacker sends a fake email or message that appears to be from a legitimate source, such as a bank or popular website.
Second, the message contains a link that directs the victim to a fake website designed to look real.Third, the victim unknowingly enters their personal information, such as login credentials or financial details.
Finally, the attacker collects this data and uses it for unauthorized access or financial fraud.
Phishing commonly targets students, working professionals, and elderly individuals who may not be fully aware of online security practices.
People who frequently use online banking or social media are especially vulnerable.
The consequences of phishing can be serious.
Victims may lose money, have their personal accounts hacked, or face identity theft. 
In some cases, attackers may use stolen information for further crimes, causing long-term damage to the victim’s reputation and financial stability.
This case highlights the importance of being cautious online, verifying sources before clicking links, and never sharing sensitive information on untrusted platforms.
